About

Teenage Ramona Lundberg's witty and hearty songwriting is at the core of the slightly absurd and 100% fun rockband “Ramona's Tea Party”. With her dad on drums and a selection of the Norwegian punk scene's most vivid characters, the quartet make for a happy bunch ready to entrance young as old with their bounce-inducing and distorted pop-rock songs. Inspirations such as Green Day, Toy Dolls and They Might Be Giants are never far away, although with female vocals and a twinkle in her eyes.

Since 2019, the group has been springing across stages all over Norway (and some Germany), leaving behind no less than glitter, sore feet and earworms. Their self-made releases include the EP “Brew-Up!” and full-length LP “Steep For X Minutes”. Song titles include “100 Washing Machines”, “British Tea Isn't Even British” and “Flossal Disaster”, that playfully touch on curiosity, self-reflection, and family relationships. “Empty Pool” is their latest heart-wrenching single.
